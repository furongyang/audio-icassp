Audio Demos for "SawSing: Singing Vocoder via Differentiable Filtered Sawtooth Wave and Gaussian Noise with fast Conformer"

Authors:

Abstract: Modern singing voice synthesis generates audios usually via a neural network-based vocoder converting spectral features predicted from musical scores to the time-domain waveforms. However, neural vocoders may suffer from phase problems that result in voice tremblings especially perceived in longer utterances of singing. Taking advantage of strong inductive biases provided in Differentiable Digital Signal Processing (DDSP), we proposed SawSing, intending to develop a DDSP-based singing vocoder that reconstructs a singing voice from a mel-spectrogram. SawSing synthesizes audio by adding the filtered sawtooth wave and the filtered noise where the filter coefficients are generated from the neural network. Benefiting from phase continuity and phase coherence, SawSing can generate favorable singing voices without glitches; it is also provided with the characteristics of lightweight and training efficiency.

The following audios are the subjective evaluation samples for our ICASSP2022 paper.
